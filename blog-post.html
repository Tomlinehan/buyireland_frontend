How to Find Your Irish Land Plot: A Complete Guide

John Beckett January 15, 2026
8 min read 1,234 views

So you've purchased your one square foot of Irish countryside - congratulations! Now you're probably wondering: how do I actually find it? This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know to locate and visit your plot in County Roscommon.

Understanding Your Documentation

When you received your BuyIreland.com package, you got several key documents that will help you find your plot:

  • Certificate of Ownership with your unique plot number
  • Deed of Assurance with mapped locations and rights-of-way
  • Detailed directions with GPS coordinates
  • Two photographs showing your land and surrounding area

These documents work together to help you pinpoint your exact square foot of Irish soil.

Using GPS Coordinates

The most accurate way to find your plot is using the GPS coordinates provided in your directions. Here's how:

  1. Locate the GPS coordinates on your directions sheet (format: 53.XXXX, -8.XXXX)
  2. Enter these coordinates into Google Maps, Apple Maps, or a dedicated GPS device
  3. The pin will drop on or very near your plot location
  4. Use satellite view to familiarize yourself with the area before your visit
Pro Tip: Download offline maps before your visit, as mobile signal can be spotty in rural County Roscommon.

Getting to County Roscommon

County Roscommon is located in the heart of Ireland, making it accessible from major cities:

  • From Dublin: Approximately 75 miles (2 hours by car)
  • From Galway: Approximately 50 miles (1 hour by car)
  • From Cork: Approximately 140 miles (2.5 hours by car)
  • From Belfast: Approximately 140 miles (2.5 hours by car)

Transportation Options

You have several ways to reach your plot:

Rental Car (Recommended): Offers the most flexibility. Book in advance and remember that Ireland drives on the left side of the road.

Private Tour: Some companies offer guided tours to your plot. Contact us for recommendations.

Public Transport + Taxi: Take a bus to the nearest town, then hire a local taxi to take you to your coordinates.

Navigating Irish Country Roads

Ireland is famous for its narrow, winding country roads. Here's what to expect:

"Irish roads have character - they'll take you through breathtaking landscapes, but they require patience and careful driving. Don't rush, and enjoy the journey." - Thomas Linehan, Co-founder

Road Tips:

  • Many rural roads are single-lane with passing places
  • Speed limits are in kilometers per hour (km/h), not miles
  • Signage can be limited - rely on your GPS
  • Watch for livestock on or near the road
  • Take your time and enjoy the scenery

Using Google Street View

Before you even leave home, you can "visit" your plot virtually:

  1. Go to Google Maps
  2. Enter your GPS coordinates
  3. Drag the yellow "Street View" person onto the map
  4. Explore the area in 360 degrees
  5. Note landmarks, gates, and nearby features

Your directions include specific Street View references showing seasonal variations of the property throughout the year.

What to Bring

Make your visit memorable and comfortable with these essentials:

Packing List:

  • Your certificate and directions (printed copies)
  • Smartphone with offline maps downloaded
  • Camera for photos
  • Waterproof jacket (Ireland's weather is unpredictable!)
  • Sturdy walking shoes or boots
  • Small flag or marker (optional, for photos)
  • Picnic supplies if you want to enjoy lunch on your land

Best Time to Visit

County Roscommon is beautiful year-round, but each season offers something different:

Spring (March-May): Wildflowers bloom, lambing season, milder weather. Perfect for photography.

Summer (June-August): Longest days, warmest weather, lush green landscapes. Most popular time to visit.

Autumn (September-November): Stunning colors, fewer tourists, harvest season. Great for peaceful visits.

Winter (December-February): Dramatic skies, possible snow, very quiet. Best for solitude seekers.

Respecting the Land

When you visit your plot, please remember:

  • Leave no trace - take all litter with you
  • Respect neighboring properties and livestock
  • Close all gates behind you
  • No building or permanent alterations permitted
  • Follow the Country Code and local regulations

Capturing the Moment

Don't forget to document your visit! Many landowners take photos with their certificate at their plot location. Share your experience with us at info@buyireland.com - we love seeing customers visit their land!
